About

I'm Kyle Orlinski. I bring with me a robust seven-year history in software development. My venture began as a front-end developer at Bon-Ton, where I specialized in web based design and functionality. As I progressed, I embraced towards a full stack development role as a consultant at Concurrency, contributing to the Modern Apps team. Alongside this, I've found immense satisfaction in indie game development, which I pursue part-time. Here, my focus is to blend my technical expertise with a creative intensity to craft captivating game experiences.

    RPG Game (6 Months)

    This was a project that I started to introduce myself to Godot's 3D. That I continued to build ontop of over some time. Then I started to pursue working on the project full-time in hopes of releasing it. I planned and tackled every system that felt needed to be introduced into the game. But as I got further and further into the development, new realizations showed themselves to me. Ultimately I hit the point where I was stretched the capabilities of my tools to their limits. It came down to the decision to move on or keep going.

    My decision was to move on to a new project. Mainly because the first start of something you will be overly eager to start creating. New systems will be built, progress is being made. Then you come to a wall of ok, now what? It almost felt like I was just building a system to build it, and not see it come fully into life. So now, my main ambition is to find a set of tools that I can rely on to help build what I intend to fully create. Currently I am in the stage of finding a new engine that I can use to help build my creations.

    Rigging - (IK Setup)

    Currently my goal when building a video game is to create a set of systems to work independently of one another. Typically the systems will lay on top of a grandeour entity such as a Character. The controls and game environment will act on these systems to be utilized. For instance, if I intend to have a looting system. Each component that makes up the system will be independent until a reaction happens to sync the components with one another. Typically in Godot and Unity I would do this using the Observer pattern. Which typically would have a built in mechanism in the engine to use. When implementing or calling.

    Instead of listing a dozen systems and what type of patterns I use to build a game. It primarily comes down to what will align with this goal of having each system be independent up until the point of a reaction to give the player new information to continue the video game. As I continue working on projects and publishing. I intend to refine this understanding and possibly re-orient to a different way of approaching game architecture.
